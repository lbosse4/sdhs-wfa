Frequently Asked Questions

Whether its about the Walk for Animals in general, or this year's Pawfect Match service, we have you covered.

FAQ for this year's

Pawfect Match Event

Do I have to regiser as a foster to take a dog out for the event?

The Humane Society takes very special care of all of our animals, so in order to take our wonderful animals out during the event, we require some standard fostering paperwork.

I'm looking to adopt but don't want to walk one of the dogs. Will there still be others at the event?

Yes! We will have a meet and greet section for our more senior dogs, and dogs that aren't quite ready yet to fly solo. Our staff will be onsite to introduce you. They work closely with the animals every day and can help you find the best fit.

What if I fall in love? Can I take my foster home?

Absolutely. The goal is to get you to fall in love with our animals as much as we have. There will be staff at the event ready to assist with the adoption process if you've found your forever best friend.

General FAQ for the

Walk for animals

Does my registration fee count towards my fundraising total?

Yes! Your registration fee, although it is not tax-deductible, will count toward your overall fundraising total. It also counts toward earning some amazing prizes.

How long is the Walk?

The course is approximately 2 miles long. There is also a 1/2 mile option for Walkers who prefer a shorter route.

Can I bring my pets?

Friendly, on-leash dogs are welcome to come! Hundreds of dogs will be in attendance, so we do not recommend bringing other types of pets.

Can my children participate?

The Walk for Animals is a family-friendly event and children are welcome to attend and enjoy the event! All participants (including children) must register online or by calling 619-243-3469. Youth participants are also welcome to fundraise for the animals.

How can my business support the Walk for Animals?

To learn more about how your business can become part of the Walk for Animals, visit our Sponsorship Opportunities page, or contact Bobbie Barnes at bbarnes@sdhumane.org or 619-243-6147. Be sure to view our Sponsorship Opportunities packet for more details on sponsorship levels and their corresponding benefits!

What if I can't attend but I want to fundraise?

Attendance on the day of the Walk is not required. If you cannot attend, you have the option to register online as a Virtual Walker and use the tools in your Participant Center to fundraise. Registered Virtual Walkers will receive a commemorative Walk for Animals T-shirt in the mail after the Walk!

How are prizes awarded to team members?

Team members qualify for fundraising prizes based on their individual fundraising achievements. Team members are encouraged to register and raise money on their own.

Prizes are redeemed individually by email after the event. Once a prize is selected for each fundraising level achieved, each walker will receive their amazing prizes by mail within 3-4 weeks. Don't forget to provide a valid email address when registering for the Walk to be able to claim prizes.

If a team raises money together by holding a team event (such as a bake sale), our Walk staff can apply the proceeds to the team's fundraising total OR proceeds can be split between team members' individual fundraising totals. Please email walk@sdhumane.org or call 619-243-3469 for assistance.

What do I do with cash and checks that I collect?

Your Walker Packet will include a donation envelope. Please bring the envelope with all cash and checks you've collected to the Walk.

If you can't attend the Walk or receive cash or checks after the event, please drop off or mail the donations in a clearly marked envelope to:

San Diego Humane Society
ATTN: Walk for Animals - San Diego
5500 Gaines St.
San Diego, CA 92110
You can keep track of check and cash donations with this donation tracking form. Please print the form, fill out completely and place it in your donation envelope, along with your collected donations, on the day of the Walk.

COVID-19

Your safety is our top priority! COVID-19 safety guidelines are subject to change and will reflect the most current recommendations from the CDC or local public health officials at the time of the event. We will continue to update you as the Walk approaches.

What do I do if I did not receive my Walker Packet?

If you registered on or after April 14 or did not receive your Walker Packet in the mail, you can pick one up at the "Preregistered" side of the Registration tent at the Walk for Animals starting at 7 a.m. If you have any questions, please contact walk@sdhumane.org.
